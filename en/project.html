1. Background

The reason of starting this project was quite simple - lack of existing tool that would answer questions like: How much car ABC from year X is really worth? How much is susipiciously low and what is way too much? In the initial version I wanted to check how prices of BMW 1 Series are distributed, as my friend was thinking about selling his. I've quickly coded webscraping from fixed url, thrown everything into dataframe and generated simple plot over the years.

On the other hand why not create a set of sources to scrape the data from, loop it all and store data in local db instead of using dataframe? Actually, no reason not to! Tadam, that's the whole story.

Having the outline of the tool, nothing stood against getting other data than prices - milage, engine size, fuel type, city and state that the offer comes from. This way we get some insight in basic but extremely interesting characteristics of polish car market.

I strongly urge you to check how distance driven per year falls with increasing age of the car - average milage of polish cars asymptotically approaches the limit of 250 thousand kilometers, unfortunately it will never reach this magical number ;)
2. How to read that chart?

The basic dashboard view shows the distribution of prices over years, for a chosen model, as a boxplot. Each box consists of three vertical lines: third quartile, median and first quartile. Whiskers come out of each box and lay 1.5 of interquartile range from each quartile.

"Wait, wait! Median? Quartile? Ranges?"

Superficial explanation for nontechnical people, that are not interested in understanding above terms:

  • median - middle line, middle value, price of an average car from certain year
  • third quartile - upper line, price is higher than 75% of values, price of a nice and well-kept car
  • first quartile - lower line, price is higher than 25% of values, price of a pretty sloppy car
  • whiskers - most values lie between them, other may be erroneous
  • outliers - suspicious prices, damaged cars, leasing reassignment, special editions etc.
3. Used tools
  • SQL Server - local db meant to store all data (including data from previous months)
  • Python - webscraping and populating the database(no API used)
